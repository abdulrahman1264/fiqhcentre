FOUNDATIONS OF ISLAMIC SCHOLARSHIP

Usul al-Fiqh & Usul al-Hadith

The two pillars of Ahlu Sunnah wal Jama'ah scholarship — how rulings and authentic narrations are established

Usul al-Fiqh — أصول الفقه

علم أصول الفقه · Roots of Islamic Jurisprudence

The science that defines how Islamic legal rulings are derived from the primary sources. It establishes the methodology scholars use to extract rulings for new situations not explicitly mentioned in scripture.

📖 Quran→ 📜 Sunnah→ 🤝 Ijma'→ ⚖️ Qiyas
4 PRIMARY SOURCES of Ahlu Sunnah Islamic Law — in order of priority
  • 1
    Quran — The word of Allah ﷻ. First and highest source of all rulings.
  • 2
    Sunnah — Authenticated sayings, actions & approvals of Prophet Muhammad ﷺ.
  • 3
    Ijma' — Scholarly consensus of the Companions and the Ummah on a ruling.
  • 4
    Qiyas — Analogical reasoning: applying a known ruling to a new analogous situation.
PRIMARY FIQH TEXTS
Al-Hidayah (Hanafi) Al-Mahalli (Shafi) Al-Risala — Imam al-Shafi'i

Usul al-Hadith — أصول الحديث

علم مصطلح الحديث · Science of Hadith Authentication

The science used to evaluate the authenticity of narrations attributed to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. It protects the Sunnah from fabrication, weak narrators, and unreliable transmission chains.

Sahih ✓→ Hasan ✓→ Da'if ⚠→ Mawdu' ✗
HADITH GRADES — only Sahih & Hasan are used for legal rulings
  • 1
    Isnad (Chain) — Every narrator identified, trustworthy, and continuously connected.
  • 2
    Matn (Text) — Content must not contradict Quran or stronger established Hadith.
  • 3
    Rijal (Narrators) — Each narrator evaluated for memory, piety, and reliability.
  • 4
    Jarh wa Ta'dil — The scholarly science of accepting or rejecting narrators.
